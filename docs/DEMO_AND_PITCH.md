# Demo and Pitch: Feasible, Desirable, Viable

Use this when presenting to investors, hackathon judges, or potential users to distinguish Aptitude from generic interview chatbots.

---

## Feasible (Technical)

Our system uses a **hidden feedback loop**: we extract strengths and weaknesses from every session, store them, and feed them back so the next session targets your gaps. We also **proactively ask context questions** at the start of each practice (e.g. what type of consulting, what you want to get out of today) and store the answers in a **structured way** so the whole product (including live interview and community) can personalize. Session focus, gathered context, and competency insights are all stored and reused across the platform.

---

## Desirable (Customer)

Users get a **clear session focus** (e.g. "This session: focusing on Quantifying Impact") so they see the product knows what to work on. They get **constructive feedback in three cards** (feedback, follow-up questions, optimized answer) instead of one long block. The learning journey **adapts to their goals and background**—we ask 1–2 short, natural questions before the first behavioral question so practice feels tailored, not random. Pre-interview profile and ongoing gathered context make each session feel part of a continuous plan.

---

## Viable (Business)

Structured user context enables **better matching with opportunities** (community sector), **stronger confidence dossiers** for applications, and a **more valuable live interview experience**. The same data drives retention and cross-feature value: one practice session improves the next, and the platform can surface relevant opportunities and prep based on what we know about the user.
